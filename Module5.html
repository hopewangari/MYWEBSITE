Sensors and Actuators

Sensors


Sensors are devices that detect physical parameters or changes in their environment and convert them into measurable signals, typically electrical.They act as the "eyes" and "ears" of a system, providing data about the world.

Types of sensors

Sensors can be categorized in several ways, including by their power requirement, means of detection, or output type Some common types include:

Temparature sensors:Measures heat and cold.
Applications: HVAC systems, medical devices (thermometers), industrial monitoring, household appliances (refrigerators, water heaters).
Pressure Sensors: Detect the force exerted by a fluid (liquid or gas).
Applications: Automotive tire pressure monitoring, blood pressure monitors, industrial equipment for predictive maintenance, weather stations.
Proximity Sensors: Detect the presence or absence of an object without physical contact.
Applications: Smartphones (screen auto-darkening during calls), automated doors, parking sensors, robotics.
Light Sensors (Photodetectors): Measure light intensity
Applications: Smartphone display brightness adjustment, camera apertures, adaptive lighting systems (car headlights, streetlights).
Humidity Sensors: Measure the moisture content in the air.
Applications: HVAC systems, weather monitoring, greenhouses, food storage
Accelerometers: Measure the rate of change of velocity (acceleration) and detect tilt or vibration.
Applications: Smartphones (orientation detection), car electronics, gaming controllers, robotics.
Position Sensors: Detect an object's position and movement. This includes linear, rotary, and tilt sensors.
Applications: Robotics, automotive systems, industrial machinery for control and operation.
Gas Sensors: Detect and measure the concentration of different gases.
Applications: Industrial safety systems, air quality monitoring, carbon monoxide detectors.
Flow Sensors: Detect the rate at which a fluid moves through a system.
Applications: Water treatment, HVAC, automotive systems, chemical industries.
Image Sensors: Convert optical images into electrical signals
Applications: Digital cameras, surveillance systems, medical imaging.

Actuators


An actuator is a component of a machine that converts energy (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, etc.) into mechanical force to produce controlled physical motion. They are the "muscles" of a system, enabling it to perform actions.
